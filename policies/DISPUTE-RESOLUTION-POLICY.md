# Dispute Resolution Policy

**Version 1.0** — April 2026

## 1. Purpose
To provide a fair, transparent, and structured way to resolve disagreements within the AIC community while protecting the project's core principles.

## 2. Scope
This policy covers disputes between:
- Contributors
- Contributors and TSC (Technical Steering Committee)
- TSC and Founder (on non-core issues)

## 3. Principles
- Good faith assumption
- Transparency (discussions should be public when possible)
- Respect for core principles (Third Path Absolute, zero-donation, ethical-from-kernel) — these are **non-negotiable**
- Founder holds final strategic authority on matters affecting the soul of the project

## 4. Resolution Process

**Step 1**: Informal discussion (via GitHub Issue or private message)

**Step 2**: Formal mediation  
- Raise a GitHub Issue with tag `dispute`  
- TSC will review and attempt mediation

**Step 3**: Escalation to Founder  
- If TSC cannot resolve, the issue goes to the Founder for final decision on core matters.

**Step 4**: Community input (optional)  
- For non-core governance issues, a 14-day public comment period may be held.

## 5. Binding Decisions
- On core principles: Founder’s decision is final.
- On technical or operational matters: TSC majority vote (with Founder veto right).

## 6. Removal from Project
Repeated bad-faith behavior, harassment, or deliberate violation of core principles may result in removal from the project after warning.

**We aim for truth-seeking and good-faith collaboration.**
